📏 Differentiation
Implicit · Parametric · Inverse trig · Partial derivatives · Higher derivatives

Key Formulae

$\frac{d}{dx}\sin^{-1}u=\frac{u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}$
$\frac{d}{dx}\cos^{-1}u=\frac{-u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}$
$\frac{d}{dx}\tan^{-1}u=\frac{u'}{1+u^2}$
Parametric: $\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$
$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\frac{(dy/dx)'_t}{dx/dt}$
Implicit: diff both sides w.r.t. $x$
D1
Implicit Differentiation
CAPE Past Paper
Given that $3y^2-4xy+\sin xy=5$, determine $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$.
💡 Hint
Differentiate every term w.r.t. $x$. For $\sin xy$: chain rule gives $\cos(xy)\cdot(y+xy')$. Collect all $y'$ terms on one side and factor.
✔ Answer
$$\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{y(4-\cos xy)}{6y-4x+x\cos xy}$$
D2
Implicit — Tangent
CAPE Past Paper
Determine the equation of the tangent to the curve $x^4-2xy+3y^2=0$ at the point $(-2,2)$.
💡 Hint
Differentiate implicitly, evaluate $y'$ at $(-2,2)$, then use $y-y_1=m(x-x_1)$.
✔ Answer
$9x-4y+26=0$
D3
Inverse Trig Differentiation
CAPE Past Paper
Differentiate $\sin^{-1}\!\left(\dfrac{e^x+a}{3}\right)$ with respect to $x$.
💡 Hint
Use $\dfrac{d}{dx}\sin^{-1}u=\dfrac{u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}$ with $u=\dfrac{e^x+a}{3}$.
✔ Answer
$$\frac{d}{dx}\sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{e^x+a}{3}\right)=\frac{e^x}{\sqrt{9-(e^x+a)^2}}$$
D4
Inverse Trig Differentiation
CAPE Past Paper
Differentiate $\cos^{-1}(3x-2)$, expressing your answer in its simplest form.
💡 Hint
$\dfrac{d}{dx}\cos^{-1}u=\dfrac{-u'}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}$. Here $u=3x-2$, $u'=3$. Expand $1-(3x-2)^2$.
✔ Answer
$1-(3x-2)^2=-9x^2+12x-3=-3(3x-1)(x-1)$
$$\frac{-3}{\sqrt{1-(3x-2)^2}}=\frac{-3}{\sqrt{-3(3x-1)(x-1)}}$$
D5
Partial Derivatives
CAPE Past Paper
Let $f=\sin(3x^2y^4)$.
  1. Show that $\dfrac{\partial}{\partial x}\sin(3x^2y^4)=6xy^4\cos(3x^2y^4)$.
  2. Given that $\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}=12x^2y^3\cos(3x^2y^4)$, determine $\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x\,\partial y}$.
💡 Hint
For (ii): differentiate $12x^2y^3\cos(3x^2y^4)$ w.r.t. $x$ using the product rule.
✔ Answer
$$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x\,\partial y}=24xy^3\cos(3x^2y^4)-72x^3y^7\sin(3x^2y^4)$$
D6
Parametric — $d^2y/dx^2$
CAPE Past Paper
Given that $x=2t-\sin t$ and $y=1-\cos t$, determine $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2}$.
💡 Hint
First find $dy/dx=\dfrac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$. Then $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\dfrac{\frac{d}{dt}(dy/dx)}{dx/dt}$.
✔ Answer
$$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}=\frac{2\cos t-1}{(2-\cos t)^3}$$
D7
Parametric — Tangent at $t=2$
CAPE Past Paper
A curve is defined parametrically by $x=(3-2t)^2$, $y=t^3-2t$. Determine the equation of the tangent at the point where $t=2$.
💡 Hint
$dx/dt=-4(3-2t)$. Evaluate slope and coordinates at $t=2$.
✔ Answer
At $t=2$: $dx/dt=4$, $dy/dt=10$, slope $=10/4=5/2$. Point: $(1,4)$.
$y-4=\dfrac{5}{2}(x-1)$, i.e., $2y=5x+3$
D8
Implicit — Complex function
CAPE Past Paper
Let $f(x,y)=4x^2+3xy^2+7x+3y$.
  1. Use implicit differentiation to show that $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=-\dfrac{8x+3y^2+7}{3(1+2xy)}$.
  2. Show that $6\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}-10=\left(\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}\right)\!\left(\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y\,\partial x}\right)+\dfrac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}$.
💡 Hint
For (ii): compute $\partial f/\partial y=6xy+3$, $\partial^2f/\partial y^2=6x$, $\partial^2f/\partial y\partial x=6y$, $\partial^2f/\partial x^2=8$. Then check both sides.
✔ Answer
Both identities confirmed. $\partial^2f/\partial y^2=6x$, $\partial^2f/\partial y\partial x=6y$, $\partial^2f/\partial x^2=8$.
∫ Integration
By parts · Reduction formulae · Partial fractions · Substitution · Definite integrals

Key Formulae

IBP: $\int u\,dv=uv-\int v\,du$
$\int\frac{f'}{f}dx=\ln|f|+c$
$\int\frac{dx}{x^2+a^2}=\frac{1}{a}\tan^{-1}\frac{x}{a}+c$
$\int\frac{dx}{\sqrt{a^2-x^2}}=\sin^{-1}\frac{x}{a}+c$
I1
Integration by Parts
CAPE Past Paper
Determine $\displaystyle\int e^{2x}\cos x\,dx$.
💡 Hint
Apply IBP twice. Let $u=\cos x$, $dv=e^{2x}dx$. After two rounds you'll have $I$ on the RHS — collect and solve.
✔ Answer
$$\int e^{2x}\cos x\,dx=\frac{e^{2x}}{5}(2\cos x+\sin x)+c$$
I2
Integration by Parts
CAPE Past Paper
Determine $\displaystyle\int e^{2x}\sin 3x\,dx$.
💡 Hint
Let $I=\int e^{2x}\sin 3x\,dx$. Use IBP twice; $e^{2x}$ is always $dv$. Collect $I$.
✔ Answer
$$\int e^{2x}\sin 3x\,dx=\frac{e^{2x}}{13}(2\sin 3x-3\cos 3x)+c$$
I3
Reduction Formula
CAPE Past Paper
  1. Derive the reduction formula: $\displaystyle\int\sin^n x\,dx=-\dfrac{1}{n}\sin^{n-1}x\cos x+\dfrac{n-1}{n}\int\sin^{n-2}x\,dx$.
  2. Hence show that $\displaystyle\int\sin^6 x\,dx=-\dfrac{1}{6}\sin^5 x\cos x-\dfrac{5}{24}\sin^3 x\cos x+\dfrac{5}{8}\int\sin^2 x\,dx$.
💡 Hint
Write $\sin^n x=\sin^{n-1}x\cdot\sin x$. IBP with $u=\sin^{n-1}x$, $dv=\sin x$. Use $\cos^2 x=1-\sin^2 x$.
✔ Answer
$I_n=-\frac{1}{n}\sin^{n-1}x\cos x+\frac{n-1}{n}I_{n-2}$
$I_6=-\frac{1}{6}\sin^5 x\cos x-\frac{5}{24}\sin^3 x\cos x+\frac{5}{8}I_2$ ✓
I4
Partial Fractions + Integration
CAPE Past Paper
  1. Use partial fractions to show that $\dfrac{x^2+7}{x^2+3x+2}=1+\dfrac{8}{x+1}-\dfrac{11}{x+2}$.
  2. Hence determine $\displaystyle\int\dfrac{x^2+7}{x^2+3x+2}\,dx$.
💡 Hint
Degrees are equal, so do polynomial division first to get $1+\text{proper fraction}$. Denom $=(x+1)(x+2)$.
✔ Answer
$$\int\frac{x^2+7}{x^2+3x+2}\,dx=x+8\ln|x+1|-11\ln|x+2|+c$$
I5
Trig Substitution
CAPE Past Paper
Using the substitution $x=4\sin\theta$, determine $\displaystyle\int\dfrac{x}{\sqrt{16-x^2}}\,dx$.
💡 Hint
$x=4\sin\theta\Rightarrow dx=4\cos\theta\,d\theta$, $\sqrt{16-x^2}=4\cos\theta$. The integral simplifies to $\int\sin\theta\,d\theta$.
✔ Answer
$$\int\frac{x}{\sqrt{16-x^2}}\,dx=-\sqrt{16-x^2}+c$$
I6
Partial Fractions + Integration
CAPE Past Paper
  1. Use partial fractions to show that $\dfrac{2x+1}{3x^2-x-2}=\dfrac{3}{5(x-1)}+\dfrac{1}{5(3x+2)}$.
  2. Hence determine $\displaystyle\int\dfrac{2x+1}{3x^2-x-2}\,dx$.
💡 Hint
$3x^2-x-2=(3x+2)(x-1)$. Cover-up method for $A$ and $B$.
✔ Answer
$x=1$: $3=5A\Rightarrow A=3/5$. $x=-2/3$: $-1/3=B\cdot(-5/3)\Rightarrow B=1/5$
$$\int\frac{2x+1}{3x^2-x-2}\,dx=\frac{3}{5}\ln|x-1|+\frac{1}{15}\ln|3x+2|+c$$
I7
Definite Integral — arctan form
CAPE Past Paper
Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_2^5\dfrac{3x}{x^2-4x+5}\,dx$.
💡 Hint
Write $3x=\frac{3}{2}(2x-4)+6$. Part 1 gives $\frac{3}{2}\ln|x^2-4x+5|$. Part 2: complete the square in denom, giving arctan.
✔ Answer
$$\int_2^5\frac{3x}{x^2-4x+5}\,dx=\frac{3}{2}\ln10+6\tan^{-1}3$$
I8
Reduction Formula — $F_n=\int(\ln x)^n$
CAPE Past Paper
Let $F_n(x)=\displaystyle\int(\ln x)^n\,dx$.
  1. Show that $F_n(x)=x(\ln x)^n-nF_{n-1}(x)$.
  2. Hence show that $F_3(2)-F_3(1)=2(\ln2)^3-6(\ln2)^2+12\ln2-6$.
💡 Hint
IBP: $u=(\ln x)^n$, $dv=dx$, $v=x$, $du=n(\ln x)^{n-1}\cdot\frac{1}{x}dx$.
✔ Answer
$F_n=x(\ln x)^n-nF_{n-1}$
$F_3(2)-F_3(1)=2(\ln2)^3-6(\ln2)^2+12\ln2-6$ ✓
I9
Partial Fractions — Repeated Quad Factor
CAPE Past Paper
  1. Show that $\dfrac{y^2+2y+1}{y^4+2y^2+1}=\dfrac{1}{y^2+1}+\dfrac{2y}{(y^2+1)^2}$.
  2. Hence evaluate $\displaystyle\int\dfrac{y^2+2y+1}{y^4+2y^2+1}\,dy$.
💡 Hint
$y^4+2y^2+1=(y^2+1)^2$. Key observation: $y^2+2y+1=(y^2+1)+2y$, so split directly.
✔ Answer
$$\int\frac{y^2+2y+1}{y^4+2y^2+1}\,dy=\tan^{-1}y-\frac{1}{y^2+1}+c$$
I10
Substitution — $e^x=3\cos\theta$
CAPE Past Paper
Using the substitution $e^x=3\cos\theta$, or otherwise, determine $\displaystyle\int e^x\sqrt{9-e^{2x}}\,dx$.
💡 Hint
$e^x=3\cos\theta\Rightarrow e^x dx=−3\sin\theta\,d\theta$ and $\sqrt{9-e^{2x}}=3\sin\theta$. Integrand becomes $9\sin^2\theta\,d\theta$ with a sign.
✔ Answer
$$\int e^x\sqrt{9-e^{2x}}\,dx=\frac{1}{2}e^x\sqrt{9-e^{2x}}-\frac{9}{2}\cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac{e^x}{3}\right)+c$$
I11
Partial Fractions
CAPE Past Paper
  1. Use partial fractions to prove that $\dfrac{2x+1}{2x^3-x^2+8x-4}=\dfrac{8}{17(2x-1)}-\dfrac{4x-15}{17(x^2+4)}$.
  2. Hence find $\displaystyle\int\dfrac{2x+1}{2x^3-x^2+8x-4}\,dx$.
💡 Hint
Factor: $2x^3-x^2+8x-4=x^2(2x-1)+4(2x-1)=(2x-1)(x^2+4)$. Write $\frac{A}{2x-1}+\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+4}$.
✔ Answer
$x=1/2$: $2=\frac{17}{4}A\Rightarrow A=8/17$. Equate to find $B=-4/17$, $C=15/17$.
$$\int\frac{2x+1}{2x^3-x^2+8x-4}\,dx=\frac{4}{17}\ln|2x-1|-\frac{2}{17}\ln|x^2+4|+\frac{15}{34}\tan^{-1}\!\frac{x}{2}+c$$
ℂ Complex Numbers
Cartesian · Modulus-Argument · Exponential form · De Moivre · Roots · Loci

Key Formulae

$|z|=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}$, $\arg z=\arctan(b/a)$
$z=re^{i\theta}=r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)$
De Moivre: $z^n=r^n e^{in\theta}$
Roots: $z_k=r^{1/n}e^{i(\theta+2k\pi)/n}$
$|z-A|=|z-B|$: perp bisector of $AB$
$|z-A|=k$: circle centre $A$, radius $k$
C1
Square Root of Complex Number
CAPE Past Paper
Calculate the square root of the complex number $2-3i$.
💡 Hint
Let $\sqrt{2-3i}=a+bi$. Square: $a^2-b^2=2$, $2ab=-3$. Also $a^2+b^2=|2-3i|=\sqrt{13}$.
✔ Answer
$$\sqrt{2-3i}=\pm\!\left(\sqrt{\tfrac{2+\sqrt{13}}{2}}-i\sqrt{\tfrac{\sqrt{13}-2}{2}}\right)\approx\pm(1.674-0.896i)$$
C2
Locus — Perpendicular Bisector
CAPE Past Paper
A point $z$ moves such that $|z-2-3i|=|4-2i-z|$.
  1. Sketch the locus of $z$ on an Argand diagram.
  2. Determine the Cartesian equation of the locus of $z$.
💡 Hint
$|z-A|=|z-B|$ means equal distance from $A=(2,3)$ and $B=(4,-2)$. Locus = perpendicular bisector of $AB$.
✔ Answer
Cartesian equation: $4x-10y=7$
C3
De Moivre — Show $(1+3i)^4=28-96i$
CAPE Past Paper
Use De Moivre's theorem to show that $(1+3i)^4=28-96i$.
💡 Hint
$r=\sqrt{10}$, $\cos\theta=1/\sqrt{10}$, $\sin\theta=3/\sqrt{10}$. Use double-angle twice to get $\cos4\theta$ and $\sin4\theta$.
✔ Answer
$(1+3i)^4=100(\cos4\theta+i\sin4\theta)=100\!\left(\frac{7}{25}-\frac{24i}{25}\right)=28-96i$ ✓
C4
Quadratic from Complex Root
CAPE Past Paper
One root of a quadratic equation is given as $4-7i$. Determine the quadratic equation with real coefficients which has the root $4-7i$.
💡 Hint
Real coefficients $\Rightarrow$ other root is $4+7i$. Form $(z-(4-7i))(z-(4+7i))=(z-4)^2+49$.
✔ Answer
Sum of roots $=8$, product $=16+49=65$
$z^2-8z+65=0$
C5
Division in Polar Form
CAPE Past Paper
Two complex numbers: $z_1=\sqrt{\frac{5}{3}}+\sqrt{5}\,i$, $z_2=2-2i$.
  1. Show that $\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}=\sqrt{\dfrac{5}{6}}\,e^{i\cdot 7\pi/12}$.
  2. Hence, without using a calculator, determine $\left(\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}\right)^2$.
💡 Hint
$\arg z_1=\pi/3$ (since $\sqrt{5}/\sqrt{5/3}=\sqrt{3}=\tan\pi/3$). $\arg z_2=-\pi/4$. Subtract arguments for division.
✔ Answer
$\left(\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}\right)^2=-\dfrac{5\sqrt{3}}{12}-\dfrac{5i}{12}$

De Moivre — $(-1+\sqrt{3}\,i)^7$

  1. Write $z=(-1+\sqrt{3}\,i)^7$ in the form $re^{i\theta}$.
  2. Hence prove that $(-1+\sqrt{3}\,i)^7=64(-1+\sqrt{3}\,i)$.
💡 Hint
$-1+\sqrt{3}\,i=2e^{i2\pi/3}$. Raise to power 7: $2^7e^{i14\pi/3}$. Reduce $14\pi/3$ mod $2\pi$.
✔ Answer
$(-1+\sqrt{3}\,i)^7=128e^{i2\pi/3}=64(-1+\sqrt{3}\,i)$ ✓

💡 Hint
$\arg(z-2)=\pi/2$ means $z-2$ is pure imaginary positive, so $\text{Re}(z)=2$.

$z=2+2\sqrt{3}\,i$
C8
De Moivre — Trig Proof
CAPE Past Paper
Using De Moivre's theorem, prove that $\dfrac{\sin5\theta}{\sin\theta}=5-20\sin^2\theta+16\sin^4\theta$.
💡 Hint
Expand $(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta)^5$ by binomial. Take imaginary part $=\sin5\theta$. Factor $\sin\theta$. Replace $\cos^2\theta=1-\sin^2\theta$.
✔ Answer
$\dfrac{\sin5\theta}{\sin\theta}=5-20\sin^2\theta+16\sin^4\theta$ ✓
C9
De Moivre — $\sin5x$ proof
CAPE Past Paper
Use De Moivre's theorem to prove that $\sin5x=16\sin^5x-20\sin^3x+5\sin x$.
💡 Hint
Expand $(\cos x+i\sin x)^5$, take imaginary part, replace all $\cos^2 x=1-\sin^2 x$.
✔ Answer
$\sin5x=5\cos^4x\sin x-10\cos^2x\sin^3x+\sin^5x$
$=\sin x(5(1-s^2)^2-10(1-s^2)s^2+s^4)$ where $s=\sin x$
$\sin5x=16\sin^5x-20\sin^3x+5\sin x$ ✓
C10
De Moivre — Square Root
CAPE Past Paper
Using De Moivre's theorem, determine the square root of $1-i\sqrt{3}$, expressing your answer in radians.
💡 Hint
$1-i\sqrt{3}=2e^{-i\pi/3}$. $\sqrt{z}=\sqrt{2}\,e^{-i\pi/6}$ (and $+\pi$ for second root).
✔ Answer
$$\sqrt{1-i\sqrt{3}}=\pm\sqrt{2}\,e^{-i\pi/6}=\pm\frac{\sqrt{6}-i\sqrt{2}}{2}$$
C11
Complex — Root of Quadratic
CAPE Past Paper
Given that $3z^2-pz+2q=0$ has root $3-5i$, determine the values of $p$ and $q$ where $p,q\in\mathbb{R}$.
💡 Hint
Real coefficients $\Rightarrow$ $3+5i$ is also a root. Use sum $=p/3$ and product $=2q/3$.
✔ Answer
Sum: $6=p/3\Rightarrow p=18$. Product: $9+25=34=2q/3\Rightarrow q=51$
$p=18,\quad q=51$
C12
De Moivre — $n$th Derivative
CAPE Past Paper
Use mathematical induction to prove that $\dfrac{d^n}{dx^n}(e^x\sin x)=2^{n/2}\,e^x\sin\!\left(x+\dfrac{n\pi}{4}\right)$.
You may use: $\sin x+\cos x=\sqrt{2}\sin(x+\pi/4)$ and $\dfrac{d^{k+1}}{dx^{k+1}}f(x)=\dfrac{d}{dx}\!\left[\dfrac{d^k}{dx^k}f(x)\right]$.
💡 Hint
Base: $n=1$, $\frac{d}{dx}(e^x\sin x)=e^x\sin x+e^x\cos x=e^x(\sin x+\cos x)=\sqrt{2}e^x\sin(x+\pi/4)=2^{1/2}e^x\sin(x+\pi/4)$ ✓
✔ Answer
By induction, $\dfrac{d^n}{dx^n}(e^x\sin x)=2^{n/2}e^x\sin\!\left(x+\dfrac{n\pi}{4}\right)$ for all $n\geq1$ ✓
